    Material Properties #

    Impulse excitation testing provides direct measurement of fundamental material properties through vibration analysis. This section explores the properties that can be determined and their physical significance.

    Overview #

    Impulse excitation testing measures the dynamic elastic properties of materials by analyzing their natural vibration frequencies. These properties are fundamental to understanding material behavior and performance.

    Property Relationships #

    Elastic Moduli #

    Young’s Modulus (E):

    • Resistance to axial deformation
    • Primary stiffness parameter
    • Temperature dependent
    • Anisotropic in some materials

    Shear Modulus (G):

    • Resistance to shear deformation
    • Related to Young’s modulus
    • Important for torsion applications
    • Material-dependent

    Poisson’s Ratio (ν):

    • Ratio of lateral to axial strain
    • Material constant
    • Range: 0 to 0.5 for most materials
    • Related to E and G

    Damping Properties #

    Quality Factor (Q):

    • Measure of energy dissipation
    • Related to material damping
    • Important for vibration applications
    • Process-dependent

    Loss Factor (η):

    • Alternative damping measure
    • Related to Q-factor
    • Material characterization
    • Process monitoring
